Data and methods

Model-based public support is derived from estimates of stated preferences for potential future vaccine mandate designs in Australia, France and Italy under mild and severe outbreak frames. Support is computed as the probability of choosing a mandate over an opt-out option.

Cost and benefit inputs are indicative and should be aligned with country-specific regulatory impact analysis guidance before formal use. Country presets allow you to restore recommended defaults or align the tool with updated national guidance.

Mandate design attributes

The discrete choice experiment asked members of the public in Australia, France and Italy to choose between potential future vaccine mandate options and an opt-out. Each option was described using the four attributes below.

A

Scope of mandate

NarrowerHigh-risk occupations only

Healthcare workers, emergency responders and similarly exposed groups must be vaccinated.

BroaderAll occupations & public spaces

Everyone working or entering public venues (cinemas, malls, restaurants, transport) must be vaccinated.

B

Exemption policy

StrictestMedical only

Only those with certified medical reasons can opt out.

ModerateMedical + religious

Exemptions for medical reasons or documented religious beliefs (with verification).

BroadestMedical + religious + personal belief

Broad opt-out allowed on medical, religious or personal grounds.

C

Coverage threshold to lift mandate

50%Half vaccinated

Mandate lifted once about half of the population is vaccinated.

70%Most vaccinated

Mandate lifted once most people are vaccinated and near-normal life can resume.

90%Near-universal

Mandate lifted only once almost everyone is vaccinated.
